Our Classes & Programs

Explore our diverse range of classes designed to inspire artistry and build technique at every level.

Recreational Program

Our Recreational Program offers quality instruction in a nurturing environment, perfect for students who wish to explore the joy of dance, build confidence, and develop valuable life skills.

Competitive Program

For the dedicated dancer, our Competitive Program provides a more intensive training experience. Dancers in this program have specific requirements, including specialized shoes and costumes, to prepare them for the highest levels of performance.

Ballet & Pointe

The foundation of all dance forms, focusing on grace, precision, and technique.

Classes Offered:

Primary Ballet (Ages 4-6), Ballet 1 (Ages 6-9), Ballet 2 (Ages 10-13), Ballet 3 (Int/Adv), Pointe (By Permission)

  • Girls: Black Leotard, Pink Convertible Tights, Pink Canvas Ballet Slippers.
  • Boys: White Tee-shirt, Black Dance Pants, Black Canvas Ballet Slippers.

Tap

Create rhythm and sound with intricate footwork and dynamic style.

Classes Offered:

Tiny Tappers (Ages 3-4), Tap 2, Tap/Jazz 2, Int/Adv Tap, Teen/Adult Tap

  • Boys: White or Color Tee-shirt, Black Dance Pants, Black Oxford-style Tap Shoes.

Hip Hop

Explore high-energy, popular dance styles in a fun and fast-paced class.

Jazz & Contemporary

Pre-Teen Jazz, Tap/Jazz 2, Int/Adv Contemporary Jazz

Please keep this information page for your reference for the year.

Registration & Showcase

Registration/Insurance Fee: A non-refundable registration fee is required for all students: $30 for a single child, or $25 per child for families with 2 to 4 students. The maximum registration fee per family is capped at $120 (for 5 or more students).

Showcase Fee: There is a MANDATORY Showcase Fee of $75/child and ($25 each additional child). This is due by your child's February tuition.

Costume Fees

Deposits: A deposit of $80.00 per dance routine is added to your November payment. Competitive Costumes are $125 per deposit.

Balances: If there is a balance it will be due in April before your child receives the costume. **Costume deposits are Non-refundable once they have been ordered.**

Studio Policies

Payment Schedule: TUITION PAYMENTS are due the first week of every month. A late fee of $10/month will be added to all payments not received by the second week of the pay period. If payment is not received by the following week, students are NOT permitted to participate in class. **Please note: Both May and June Tuition are due with MAY payment.**

Payment Methods: Payments for registration, showcase, and tuition can be made by cash, money order, debit, or credit card. Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover are accepted.

Tuition Policy: Tuition is based on a flat course fee and remains the same regardless of attendance. Tuition is not affected by school closings due to holidays and inclement weather conditions.

Private Lessons: All private lessons (Solos, Duets and Trios) must be prepaid prior to scheduling. A 24hour cancellation notice due prior to scheduled lesson.

Sibling Discount: Family sibling discounts are available for siblings in the same household!

Referral Bonus: Refer a friend who registers for classes and receive $50.

Class Policies: If a student is tardy, it's at the instructor's discretion whether to allow the student to join the class already in progress. Trial classes may be requested for students interested in new techniques, however, trial classes DO NOT hold a space in the class.

Withdrawal: Please give the office written notice of withdrawal from class(es). Failure to contact the office within 30 days of registering will result in the student being billed for the entire year (including costume and showcase fees).

Communication & Closings: Each child has their own file in the lobby. Please check your family file REGULARLY for newsletters, receipts, and invoices. We also send information via email. To notify us of student's illness or absence, you may email: info@dance4capa.com or call (716) 573-6288. Emergency cancellations/closings due to inclement weather will be announced on local television stations, Channels 2, 4, and 7 in addition to being posted on, www.dance4capa.com, Facebook, and the studio phone, (716) 573-6288.

Welcome to Clark Academy!

We want to take this opportunity to thank you for joining us for the 2025-2026 Dance Season. We know that your child will have an enjoyable, educational and memorable experience here at the Academy. Clark Academy offers quality instruction by skilled professionals in the theatrical performing arts through an extensive variety of performance-oriented programs. Clark Academy cultivates a unique learning environment for the diverse population of children and adults who participate and contribute in the cultural life in the Western New York and Southern Ontario communities.

Classes are available in Ballet, Tap, Jazz, Musical Theater, Hip Hop, Acrobatics, Modern, African, Contemporary, and Caribbean.

A Message from Matthew: Why Should My Child Dance?

In the early years of a child's life it is important to introduce them to activities that stimulate their development of physical and mental capacities. They are like a "sponge". The ability to emulate what they see, hear and develop their memory embellished by the introduction to dance. It is a necessary progression towards coordination, social interaction, and physical stability. The development of their retention skills are enhanced by learning to dance. Discipline is developed by learning a regiment, repetition of techniques, learning to follow directions, working with others, and confidence building, are all beneficial life skills that contribute to child's self esteem growth. Peaking their interests and creating an outlet for them to explore their creative and conceptual sides of the brain, is the major reason why YOUR child should Dance!

Clark Academy Code of Conduct

At the Clark Academy of Performing Arts we strive to develop not only excellent dancers, but most importantly, exceptional citizens. Once enrolled at the Clark Academy, the dancer and his/her family become members, and as such are asked to conduct themselves in a manner that upholds the traditions of the Clark Academy, as well as demonstrate respect for themselves and for others. Therefore, we ask All DANCERS enrolling in the Clark Academy and FAMILY MEMBERS adhere to the following:

  1. respect your self
  2. respect others
  3. behave in a manner that demonstrates the above both in and out of the studio

Anyone who violates this code of conduct will be subject to removal from the Clark Academy of Performing Arts by the Artistic Director. If such removal occurs, no refunds of any type will be given.

All students should be properly dressed with their hair secured up/back away from their face and neckline. Students who do not come to class properly attired will be denied entrance.
