The Benefits of Dance

More than just movement, dance is a powerful tool for growth, confidence, and lifelong skills.

Enhanced Brain Power

Remembering steps and focusing on rhythm is a fantastic exercise for the brain, improving concentration and academic performance.

Emotional Well-being

Dancing releases endorphins, the "feel-good" chemicals that can help ward off depression and anxiety.

Physical Health

Improve flexibility, posture, and weight management in a fun, active environment that's accessible to everyone.

Life Skills & Perseverance

Dance fosters perseverance as students learn from mistakes, strengthen sensory awareness, and interpret body language.

A Nurturing Community

We provide a "dance home" where students feel supported by teachers and peers, building friendships that last a lifetime.

Our Founder

Matthew Clark

A World-Renowned Dancer, Choreographer, and Mentor

A Legacy of Excellence

Matthew Clark's journey is one of sustained artistic excellence and international acclaim. From his debut as a "brilliant young tap dancer" at the 1991 IDO World Tap Dance Championship to receiving New York's prestigious "Living Legend Award 'Life as an Artist'," his career is a testament to his dedication. His profound influence is recognized through inductions into The Canadian Dance Hall of Fame and the Dance Teachers Hall of Fame, and commendations from President Bill Clinton for his contributions to the arts.

The ultimate artistic endorsement came from the legendary Gregory Hines, who lauded Matthew's work as "Fabulous footwork and choreography."

Architect of Champions

Matthew's genius extends beyond his own performance to his incredible ability to cultivate success in others. As the coach and choreographer for both the US and Canadian National Teams, he has led his students to an astounding 21 World Tap Dance Championship titles. His proven record of producing results at the highest levels of international competition makes him one of the most sought-after instructors in the world.

A Philosophy of Mentorship

Beyond the trophies and accolades, Matthew is committed to creating a "dance home"—a nurturing and supportive environment where students can thrive. His teaching philosophy centers on using dance as a vehicle to build confidence, unlock potential, and foster personal growth. This commitment to developing the whole person is at the heart of Clark Academy, where every student is encouraged to become not only a great dancer but a great individual.
